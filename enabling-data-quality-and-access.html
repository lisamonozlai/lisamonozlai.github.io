A Lightweight Approach to Data Quality and Access

A hypothetical scenario showing how a small team can standardize data collection, build a lightweight relational database, and design a simple UI to improve data quality and access.

A Portfolio Project | By Lisa Monozlai

Overview

This case study shows how a small team can strengthen data quality and access by standardizing inputs, linking spreadsheets into a relational model, and exposing the data through a simple UI. The approach helps teams:

  • Collect data consistently using shared Excel templates
  • Integrate multiple spreadsheets into a coherent relational structure
  • Explore and update records through a user friendly interface

Scenario

A small engagement team tracks organizations, contacts, and interactions across several programs, each using its own spreadsheet with different formats and naming conventions. As the team grows, answering basic questions requires manual cleanup and stitching data together. With no dedicated data engineer or CRM budget, the team needs a simple and maintainable solution.

Solution

The team can improve data quality and access by following three practical principles that enable consistent inputs, create connected records, and make information easy to update and explore through an interactive database.

Results

By combining standardized templates, a relational schema, and a simple UI, the team can move from scattered spreadsheets to a coherent engagement picture. Staff can answer questions faster, trust that records are consistent, and maintain the system without specialized technical support.
